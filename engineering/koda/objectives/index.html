Nivren programme

Engineering objectives

Nivren's objectives are organised by maturity so design intent is never presented as completed capability.

CurrentLast reviewed: 15 July 2026Publisher: YewForge Digital

Immediate engineering objectives

Truthful operational visibility

Show available local state as live, historical, unavailable or fixture-derived rather than substituting convenient data.

Bounded interaction proofs

Test interface behaviours in small prototypes before treating them as desktop-level commitments.

Traceable research machinery

Maintain sessions, append-only events and recovery checks before attempting future campaigns.

Human final authority

Keep approvals, constraints and recovery decisions explicit rather than framing the system as self-authorising.

Active objectives

Active work is directed towards an inspectable Workshop, a clear Shard interface language, read-only observability, and reliable local developer tooling. The programme also keeps external memory and evidence records separate from casual conversational claims, so important status can be grounded in documented sources.

Privacy and reliability objectives are practical constraints: local-first operation, explicit provenance, recoverable state, bounded dependencies and a distinction between current runtime activity and replayed records.

Public and private boundary

This journal describes engineering lessons, status and evidence. It does not publish raw datasets, sealed evaluation material, voice references, credentials, private operational topology, personal records or security-sensitive implementation detail.
