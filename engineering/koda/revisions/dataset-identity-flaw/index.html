Revision Record KR-001

Dataset Identity Coupled Meaning to Row Metadata

ResolvedSystem: Dataset FoundryLast reviewed: 15 July 2026

Initial objective and assumption

Dataset qualification sought to establish provenance and split discipline for bounded research material. An earlier identity approach allowed meaning to be coupled to row, campaign or generation metadata, risking an invalid measure of semantic separation.

Original assumption, test and root cause

The original assumption was that row-linked identity could stand in for semantic identity. The contradiction was found through qualification analysis; the root cause was coupling meaning to incidental metadata.

Failure or contradiction and design response

Qualification analysis identified the coupling. The design response used a corrected semantic envelope, regenerated material, balanced cross-split grammar representation and independent comparison. The distinction between Dataset Foundry R0 and later R1.1 research material remains explicit.

Validation after correction

Defined regeneration, provenance, disjointness and holdout checks supported qualification of bounded R1.1 corpus and holdout material. This is qualification evidence only.
